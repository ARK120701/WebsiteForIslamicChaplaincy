About Us

Our Story & Mission

Born on the hospital floor, shaped by service, and committed to growing the next generation of Muslim chaplains.

Our Origin

Where We Come From

NMCA National Muslim Chaplains Association was founded by three Muslim chaplaincy professionals who met during their training at Northshore University Hospital. Every day, walking from room to room — sitting with patients in their fear, their grief, their uncertainty — they saw the same thing: the need for Muslim spiritual care was immense, and the number of qualified Muslim chaplains to meet it was far too small.

They also saw something else: countless Muslims in their communities with the calling, the compassion, and the dedication to do this work, but with no clear path forward. No one had explained Clinical Pastoral Education to them. No one had told them what accreditation looks like, what a residency involves, or what a career in chaplaincy can be.

This organization exists to be that guide — the resource, the community, and the mentorship network they wish had existed when they started.

Mission

What We Stand For

✨ Faith-Rooted Service

We believe caring for people in their most vulnerable moments is an act of worship. Chaplaincy is fard kifayah — a communal obligation — and we treat it as such.

📚 Professional Excellence

We hold ourselves to the highest professional standards because the people we serve deserve nothing less. CPE accreditation is not optional — it is the foundation.

🌐 Inclusive Representation

American Muslims are diverse — in ethnicity, language, madhab, and background. Our organization welcomes and celebrates that full diversity.

🤝 Communal Support

No chaplain walks alone. We build a network where those in training and those in practice support, advise, and lift one another.

The Gap We Are Closing

The Need Is Real

Muslims are among the fastest-growing religious groups in the United States, yet are dramatically underrepresented in professional chaplaincy roles.
